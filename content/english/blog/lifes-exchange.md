A new year begins.

The afternoon of December 31, 2008, was very quiet. There were fewer friends on MSN than usual, and the phone, which normally rang off the hook, had fallen silent. Few people remained in the office. The young ones had left early — either catching the early shuttle home or meeting friends for shopping. They left with such eagerness and haste that they did not even rinse their teacups or cap their pens.

I looked carefully around my office, at my desk. I wanted to leave early too, yet I could not bear to. What a lovely afternoon this was — after so many days, there had rarely been one so peaceful.

Life is like this: it gives you one thing and takes another. You gain thought, but lose happiness. You gain experience, but lose innocence. You gain obligations, but lose time. We are constantly exchanging with life, trading what we think is unimportant for what we think matters. In the end, we may discover that what we gave away was the most precious of all.
