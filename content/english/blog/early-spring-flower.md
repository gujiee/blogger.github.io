A tiny flower

born on the early spring earth.

A tiny pink flower.

A tiny green stem.

Tiny, round leaves.

Lying on the earth about to awaken.

The pink flower is like the earth's heart,

shivering in the cold wind.

Like the heart of the waking earth, trembling.

The early spring wind is so cold, so cold.

It freezes tears from the pink flower.

It freezes the tender green stem, curling back into the ground.

It freezes the round leaves, bowing their heads.

On the vast early spring earth:

only the soil, about to awaken;

the tall, stubborn stones;

the bare trees.

Only when the early spring wind —

carrying its chill — blows past,

can you see a pink flower,

its frozen, tear-streaked face peeking out,

searching for the dream of spring.
