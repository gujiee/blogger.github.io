*January 5 — Carefree*

Many Christians live anxious, worried lives — some so troubled that it is pitiful. How to find complete rest amid the busyness of daily life is a secret worth knowing.

Let us first ask: what use is worry? Worry has never made anyone stronger. It has never helped anyone do God's will. It has never helped anyone escape difficulty. Worry only damages a useful life, a beautiful life.

Anxiety, worry, concern — these are absolutely forbidden by our Lord. He said: "Do not worry about what you will eat or drink or wear." He did not mean that we should not plan ahead, or that we should live without any structure. He meant that we should not be anxious about these things.

Do you think others cannot see your worry? I tell you: from the furrows on your brow, from the tone of your voice, from the smallest gestures of your daily life — from your lack of joy — others already know.

We should cast our cares upon God! For He said: "Come to me, all you who are weary and burdened, and I will give you rest."

— Charles Sheldon

Worry, anxiety, doubt, distrust — these are the Christian's greatest weaknesses. What do we gain from them? Do they not often rob us of our opportunities? Do they not often steal our wisdom and decisiveness? Many times we could have floated on faith, but sank because of our own struggles.

Oh, how we need the power of stillness! Oh, let us be still, and know that He is God! The Holy One of Israel cannot fail to protect and save His own people. Every word He speaks is effective. Mountains may be moved, heaven and earth may pass away, but His word will not pass away. He is worthy of our trust, and deserves our trust.

O my soul, return! Return and rest! Return and fall into the arms of the Lord!

— Mrs. Charles Cowman

I am deeply grateful for Cowman's *Streams in the Desert*, which has accompanied me through so many days and nights. I carried it with me, always ready to listen to the voice from heaven. It was like a lamp in the dark night, guiding those who had lost their way, driving away confusion, worry, and uncertainty.
