You are charming and alluring,

decked in dazzling jewels.

You are soft and fragile,

always passing time with crystal tears.

You are young,

yet count your days one by one.

You are beautiful and bountiful,

yet stir a quiet sorrow.

You smile —

a fading sunset in the rain.

You weep —

and all the world grieves with you.

I want to leave you,

but you draw closer day by day,

with your tears and your tenderness,

breaking my resolve.

And when I stay with you,

I find my heart aging in sorrow,

my tears washing the sky.

Do not adorn yourself with withered yellow flowers.

Do not wear tattered leaves as your cloak.

Do not always dress in faded gray.

Do not always show your tearful face.

Though all things fade,

there are still buds unfurling,

new leaves growing,

bright garments to be worn.
