Yesterday morning at Brown Bay, I saw a flock of wild geese flying over the sea in a V-formation, their calls fading into the distance. That evening, I received a WeChat message from my father — a poem he had copied onto his small blackboard. My father does not send me messages often. It was a poem by Tao Yuanming. A fine poem, but tinged with melancholy. I could not bear that melancholy, so I wrote a reply.
