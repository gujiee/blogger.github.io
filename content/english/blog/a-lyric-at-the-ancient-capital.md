Small parasol leaves, faint gardenia blossoms,

drizzling rain locks the green hills in haze.

Ancient Yue wine is strong,

the Torreya nuts are fragrant,

the shallow riverbank stretches on —

they say this is where Xishi once washed silk.

Set to the tune *Man Jiang Hong* — Remembering the Ancient Capital of Yue

Mist and rain in haze,
Where I gaze — green hills knit their brows like sorrow.
I remember, beside the Huan River,
gauze robes and shadows drifting.

Parasol leaves hold pearls, shaking fragments of jade;
gardenias cradle dew, spilling fragrant drops.
Ask the gray waves — how many ancient things have sunk?
None remain to tell.

Yue wine is fierce — a thousand vats overflow.
Torreya nuts ripen — ten thousand bundles piled high.
Look at the cold river stones,
still carved with frost-bitten footprints.

Mighty deeds have gone with the gulls and waves;
a fragrant name lives on in woodcutters' songs.
By the evening clouds — who returns, washing white silk,
among the empty bamboo grove?
