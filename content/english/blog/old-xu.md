Before 8:30 yesterday, I was already at the office. The door was still locked — I usually do not carry a key — so I waited in the cold wind for more than ten minutes until Wanhua arrived. He was surprised to see me so early. "What's going on?" he asked. "Old Xu, the one I often mention, is coming today."

Old Xu is one of my suppliers. We have known each other for nearly four years. In truth, I have not bought much from him — less than half a million in total. Yet I value him greatly and hold him in high respect.

He is not old; just a few years older than me, and he looks very young, dressed quite fashionably. Sitting in his spacious Crown on the way to Quanjude Roast Duck, he spoke with sincere contentment, in his Southern accent: "Hey, I never had much schooling. Came from the countryside. My dream was always just to drive a car for someone — no wind, no sun. Now I have my own car. I'm satisfied."

This reminded me of three years ago, when Old Xu bought his first car and insisted on driving me from Anhui to Yangzhou. It was April in the Jiangnan region, a fine rain falling. In his new Corolla, he said the same thing.

Old Xu is well known among his peers. His fame comes not from his wealth, but from his story.

He is a country man. He married young. To make a living, he became a salesman — selling police equipment, by his own account. From his other friends, I gathered that what he really sold was pirated CDs. That was 1992. The same year, I was still in university, enjoying the simple joys of student life, studying the classics. Old Xu, almost exactly my age, was standing on street corners with a tattered cloth full of loose CDs, shivering in the wind and rain, his heart in his throat. What a contrast. It humbles me every time I think of it.

Old Xu's breakthrough came around 1998. His opportunity came from a childhood friend, a neighbor from the village. When state-owned enterprises restructured, this friend acquired a small factory that produced industrial goods, and Old Xu became his most capable assistant. In this way, Old Xu transformed from a street vendor into a sales manager. In 2001, he closed a deal worth tens of millions — a project that brought enormous growth to the factory and a generous reward to him. Whenever he talks about that deal, he becomes emotional, describing every detail with passion. That project gave him two years of life in the North, changed his rice-eating habits, gave him a stubborn stomach condition — and also gave him a丰厚 return and the confidence to grow.

Later, Old Xu bought land, invested in real estate, and built his own factory, producing his own products. I know from experience how difficult it is to build a factory when you do not have abundant capital. But Old Xu, every time we meet, still says the same thing: "I'm very satisfied. I have a good life now." Plain and contented.

Whenever I face a difficulty, I find myself calling Old Xu. I never tell him what the problem is. I just chat with him, listen to his contented rambling, and somehow, in that rambling full of satisfaction with life, my own troubles begin to feel lighter.
