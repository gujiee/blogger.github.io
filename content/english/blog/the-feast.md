After a long day's work, I come home and want nothing more than to sit quietly at my desk, light a lamp — not too bright, just enough to see the words before me — with a cup of light tea and a book that comforts the soul.

After a while, I close the book and sit in stillness, listening to the sounds of nature outside the window: the wind, the rain, the falling leaves, the low chirping of insects, and the silent communion with the unseen spirit.

Stillness is the origin of all things, and the end of all things. In stillness, the heart can communicate with the vast universe and connect with the ancients who wander through this boundless space.

In this lamplight, what appears is not dim, unclear words, but clear, transparent thoughts — drifting like light clouds through the cosmos.

Deep night, without a trace of human presence, all sounds silenced — that is when my heart is most at ease, most joyful, least lonely. That is my feast.
