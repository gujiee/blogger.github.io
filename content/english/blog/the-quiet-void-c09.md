Next came a middle-aged man with a thin face, dark skin, small eyes, and a sparse, untrimmed beard. His long hair stuck out from under a blue worker's cap. He wore a blue work suit, both somewhat worn and not very clean.

The young man asked, "What is your name?"

The man answered, "Erbao. Li Erbao. Everyone calls me Erbao."

"Age?"

"Thirty-eight."

The man fumbled uncomfortably in the pocket of his work shirt. The young man said, "Li Erbao, no smoking in here." The man's hand retreated awkwardly.

"Occupation?"

"What?"

"What work do you do?"

"Worker."

"What kind of work?"

The man's hand reached for his pocket again, then jerked back as if shocked. He answered, "I'm a temporary worker. Mainly trimming branches. There are a few roads in the forest farm that lead to the main road — they're narrow, and branches often block them. Sometimes felled trees block the way too. They have to be cleared, or vehicles can't get through."

"Where were you on the night of November 30?"

"In the dormitory at the forest farm."

"Do you often stay in the dormitory?"

"August and September, yes."

"Why August and September?"

"That's the busy season for trimming. A day's work is never enough, so I stay in the dorm."

"When you don't stay in the dorm, where do you live?"

"I live at home, in Shichang Village nearby. Twenty minutes by bus."

"Why did you stay in the dorm on November 30?"

"There was some work to do at the forest farm that day. I finished in the afternoon. Old Liao from the farm asked me to play cards, so I stayed. Then another person came, said let's play mahjong. We played mahjong until late, so I didn't go home."

"What time did you stop playing mahjong?"

"After two. It was too cold, so I wanted to warm up in bed."

"What about the others?"

"They didn't sleep. They kept playing cards."

"Did you fall asleep?"

"Yes. I had some drinks while playing, so I fell asleep as soon as I hit the bed."

"What time did you wake up?"

"After three."

"Were you woken by a noise?"

"No. Old Liao and the others woke me up."

"Why?"

"They said there was a noise outside."

"Did you hear it?"

"Yes. It was very loud. The bed was shaking."

"Did you see anything?"

"I pulled open the curtain. I saw two lights. Couldn't make them out clearly. The windows were covered in steam."

"Had you heard a sound like that before?"

"No. It was very loud."

"What did the two lights look like?"

"Couldn't see clearly. The glass was all steamy."

"Do you usually drink?"

"A little."

"How much did you drink on the night of November 30?"

"Old Liao had about a catty of loose liquor. I finished most of it. He mostly drank it."

"Do you know what UFOs are?"

"What?"

"Do you know what flying saucers are?"

"Yes. They were those two lights that night."

Finally, the man signed the paper that was pushed toward him. He casually put the pen into his coat pocket. Only when the young man reminded him did he awkwardly take it out, place it back on the table, laughed sheepishly, and left.
