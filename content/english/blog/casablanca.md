The sun was beautiful today. My office was warm and cozy, and my first order of 2009 was nearly confirmed — a good omen, I hoped. At last, I could relax.

I opened my music folder and double-clicked *Casablanca*. The melody began, slowly rising through the sunlit office, winding around the leaves of the pothos, climbing the spider plant on the cabinet, until the small room was filled.

This is my favorite song — my only song, really. I remember hearing it for the first time at a Starbucks near the Jianguo Hotel. It was early winter, already cold and windy. The pagoda tree leaves along Chang'an Avenue were stubbornly hanging on, but some had withered and fallen, blown by the wind to who knows where. My friend and I sat by the window, ordered two cappuccinos — the only coffee I can drink — and slowly sipped as we chatted.

The day was cold and gray. Pedestrians wrapped their coats tightly around themselves, heads down, hurrying past the window. The cold wind followed customers through the thick door curtain, like a mischievous child slapping our faces with icy little hands.

But inside, it was wonderfully warm, filled with the rich aroma of coffee. We talked and drank, and before we knew it, dusk had arrived. Slanting rays of sunset fell on my face, warm and comforting. Then I heard it — a soft melody: *Casablanca*.

I did not understand the lyrics. I simply loved it. It was love at first hearing. I cannot sing. I have no ear for music. Once, Weiwei took me to a symphony concert, and I fell asleep amid the叮当 sounds. But *Casablanca* I remembered instantly, deeply, forever.

*Casablanca* makes me think of white houses under a tropical sun, beside blue-green water; the silhouettes of tall palm trees against the setting sun; a breezy harbor; colorful lights glowing after dusk; and a passionate Spanish dancer.

*Casablanca* also makes me think of cold weather, warm sunlight, and fragrant coffee.

And more than anything, *Casablanca* makes me think of a spinning red skirt, a flash of longing in bright eyes, distant clouds of yellow dust, and an unbearable longing that climbs from the brow into the heart.
