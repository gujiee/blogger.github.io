When did you come into this world?
And when did you crawl into my heart?
I curse you.
You ghost.

You hollowed out my soul,
left it wandering
in darkness alone.

No more warmth.
No more joy.

Leave me now.
You ghost.

I want to live with joy.
I want to do something with this life.
Not waste it away.

---

*Loneliness:*

I do not know where I come from.
I do not know where I am going.
I am a ghost,
drifting through the night sky.

There are so many people in this world.
They are young and strong,
yet they waste their years.
They have boundless futures,
yet they wander in confusion.
They should learn more,
yet they only play.
Their hearts are meant to be warm,
yet they grow cold.

They are my prey.
I feed on their years.
I secrete sorrow.
They will grow old,
while I grow younger every day.

---

Go, Loneliness.
I am not one of those people.
I may have no grand ambitions,
but I am not without diligence.
I may have no splendid future,
but I walk my path with confidence.
I may be fond of play,
but I still learn.
I may carry sorrow,
but my heart has never frozen over.

Go, Loneliness.
I am not the one you are looking for.

---

*Loneliness:*

Words are merely froth upon the air.
Only the heart is real.

I dwell within your heart.
I speak with your soul.

You are diligent,
yet you cannot persevere.
You study,
yet you are careless and lax.
You have not frozen over,
yet you are steeped in sorrow.

I do not understand.
Where does your sorrow come from?
I do not understand.
Why should youth be so sorrowful?

---

*Me:*

I am sorrowful.

When autumn has arrived.
When the autumn rain brushes down the first leaf.
When the autumn wind plucks the last flower.

I am sorrowful.

When a solitary bird shivers in the rain.
When bare branches sway in the wind.
When autumn has arrived.

I grieve for the fallen leaf that can never return to the tree.
I grieve for the little bird — does it have a warm home?
