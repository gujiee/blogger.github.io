I wrote a short piece in my spare time, and Changhong called me a "thinker" for it.

But the truth is, like Changhong, I too am sick of thinkers. And I have my reasons.

In university, there was a famous student at Beihang — very famous (I have forgotten his name). Everyone in the Eight Colleges knew him. He always wore a classical hat, sported a little beard, and his most distinctive feature was a walking stick he never let go of. A walking stick was, of course, useless for a young man in his twenties. But it gave him an excuse to associate himself with Nietzsche. That was when I learned about the thinker who proclaimed himself the sun.

I admired people with ideas back then. I scoured every philosophy book in the library — Hegel, Kant, Nietzsche, Marx, Laozi, Wang Yangming. But sadly, I understood nothing and remembered nothing.

When I started working, I found myself thinking during idle moments: How should one live? What happens after death? — the kind of questions that occupy a bored mind. As the lyric goes: "The young know not the taste of sorrow, so they force themselves to write of it." And with sorrow came the search for joy. I read Laozi, Zhuangzi, Confucius, Guiguzi, hoping to find answers. To make life happier, more fulfilling. But the more I read, the more questions I had. And the more questions I had, the more I wanted to read. And so I spent the most无聊 two and a half years of my life.

Laozi walked out of Tong Pass and disappeared. Confucius wandered his whole life, never recognized. Nietzsche was the sun — he burned himself out, but brought no light to the world. Marx spent a lifetime describing a beautiful world, only for Gorbachev to sweep it all away.

I hope I have anything — but not ideas.
