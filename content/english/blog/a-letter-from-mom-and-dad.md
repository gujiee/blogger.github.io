Dear Son,

We received your letter and were so happy to hear from you! You told us so much about your daily life over there — that's exactly what we were hoping for. When we think about it, what an incredible journey it's been — crossing thousands of miles of mountains and seas to reach a foreign land (truly thousands of miles of mountains and seas!). Everything was different, especially the language — and yet you overcame it all. You found a school, you found housing, all on your own. To us, that's truly remarkable. From your letter, it sounds like life is treating you well.

First of all, it seems like getting your child into school was quite straightforward. Look at you — you'd barely arrived, and before long she was already studying! Compared to Beijing, isn't that much simpler? We're curious — does the elementary school in Auckland have many requirements for enrollment? Or is it like China, where every child has to be filed into a computer database and tightly controlled before they can start school?

We also received several photos from you today. The one of our precious granddaughter is lovely, haha! By "lovely" we mean we can really see her clearly. She looks strong and healthy — though her hair is a bit long. You should comb it for her, or cut it short. Right now, Liu Huichao is the one who combs Mengmeng's hair — she puts it in a ponytail. Since Yangyang is busy with work and his office is far from home, it falls to him as a father to braid his daughter's hair. Over there, since Bao Bao's mother is busy with her studies, you'll have to take on more responsibility and learn a few new skills. Taking good care of your daughter is the most important task right now!

When you think about it — all the effort you've put in, all the hardships you've endured — isn't it all for the sake of giving your child a better upbringing? Isn't it all so that she can live a happier life?

We're glad to hear you have a car now, and that you can pick her up from school for lunch. That's great! At the same time, we know it means extra work for you. But for the sake of your child, it's worth the extra effort. However — you must also take care of your own health! You are the pillar of your family. Let us remind you here: in the midst of all your busyness, please pay attention to your well-being. Back in Beijing, whenever work got hectic, I noticed a few dark patches on your cheeks. I mentioned it to you — that's a sign of exhaustion! Your father used to have the same thing — when he was under pressure from work, not just his cheeks but even his forehead would develop dark patches (now that he's retired, they're gone). So we urge you: make your health your top priority!

Now that you have come to believe in the Lord — that has set our minds at ease. Truly, all our actions should be guided by the Lord; don't charge ahead blindly. When you feel unhappy, when you face difficulties in life or work, call upon the Lord. In short, don't be reckless. Before you act, think carefully, pray earnestly, and seek God's will. If God clears the path, go ahead; if God blocks the way, don't. Walk this way and you will be steady, and you will have peace and joy. Well, I won't say much more — you have already experienced God's grace and love for yourself.

It's been almost three months since you arrived in Auckland. What do you think — what are the advantages and disadvantages of Beijing versus Auckland? Also, we think that since you bought that land in Vanuatu, just let it be. Don't rush to build a house on it. Someday selling the land could be a nice source of income. And another thing: how far do you live from the train station? How many kilometers? In which direction? We saw the train stations on the map of Auckland. Also, Auckland University has several campuses — which one is Weiwei at? Is it far? Haha — don't mind us asking so many questions!

Take another photo of the side of your pickup truck for us.

We're doing fine here.

Love,
Mom and Dad

April 19, 2015
