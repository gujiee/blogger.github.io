The video room fell silent. The old projector hummed quietly in the corner. Gu Yuan sat motionless, staring at the blank screen.

He had just watched the first classified video — something that was not supposed to exist, something that the institute had never officially acknowledged. And yet he had seen it with his own eyes.

The tape had no label, only the number 7 written on a yellowed sticker. Inside the case was a note: "Restricted — Level 7 clearance required." Gu Yuan's clearance was Level 6. But the video room had been empty, the cabinet unlocked, and curiosity had gotten the better of him.

The footage was grainy, clearly shot in low light. It showed a large, hangar-like room. In the center of the room was an object — roughly disc-shaped, about 5 meters in diameter, with a smooth, metallic surface. There were no visible seams, no rivets, no markings. A group of people in white lab coats stood around it, taking measurements, murmuring to one another.

The camera moved closer. The object's surface seemed to absorb light rather than reflect it. It was a deep, matte gray, like the color of a storm cloud. One of the lab coats reached out and touched it, then pulled his hand back quickly, as if burned.

"You okay?" someone asked.

"It's cold," the lab coat replied. "Very cold. Like dry ice."

A voice from behind the camera: "Have you identified the material?"

"No. It doesn't match anything in our database. It's not steel, not aluminum, not titanium. It's not anything we know."

The camera zoomed in on the surface. At maximum magnification, it showed a pattern — faint, almost invisible lines that formed a geometric design unlike anything Gu Yuan had ever seen. It was not a circle, not a triangle, not a square. It was something in between.

"Is this writing?" someone asked.

"Too regular to be natural," another voice replied. "But we can't read it. It doesn't correspond to any known language."

The video ended.

Gu Yuan sat in the dark, his heart pounding. He thought of the object in his bag — the small, unmarked plaque he had found in the classified document room. He had assumed it was some kind of key or token. But now he wondered: was it connected to this? Was it from the same place as the disc-shaped object?

He pulled out the plaque and held it up to the dim light. It was cool to the touch, smooth, featureless. But as he turned it in his fingers, the light caught it at a certain angle, and for a moment, he could have sworn he saw a pattern — the same geometric pattern from the video.

He blinked, and it was gone.

He sat in the darkness, staring at the plaque, waiting for the pattern to reappear.

But the room remained silent, and the plaque remained blank.
