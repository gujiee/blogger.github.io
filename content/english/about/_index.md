My name is J. Gu. This blog is my personal space for words — fragments of life, travel, poetry, and distant horizons.

I was born in a small city surrounded by mountains in northern China. I lived and worked in Beijing, and later moved to the South Pacific island nation of Vanuatu, where I lived on Dolphin Road, survived Cyclone Pam, and built my first house with my own hands on a foundation of coral stone. Today, my family and I live in Torbay, on the North Shore of Auckland, New Zealand — a place gently wrapped by the sea and sandy beaches.

I wouldn't call myself a writer. Just someone who, when sleep won't come in the deep of night, gets up and puts down a few lines. I started writing poetry when I was young — over thirty years now, from the autumn of China Agricultural University all the way to the autumn of the Southern Hemisphere. I wrote about loneliness, dreams, the small flowers of early spring, and the fading light of autumn afternoons. Later I began writing essays, recording the roads I have traveled, the people I have met, the islands I have lived on, the papayas and king bananas I have eaten.

My daughter learned fluent French at a French school in Vanuatu. I learned to live on "island time." We had a cat named Maomao and a dog named Peppa — Peppa was once bitten on the head by a larger dog, and we stayed up with her all night. These small, warm, sometimes messy moments make up my most precious memories of the South Pacific.

Who am I? Someone who once worked in an office in Beijing. Someone who built a house on the equator. Someone who learned to slow down by the sea in Auckland. A husband, a father, a son — in May 2026, I traveled halfway across the world to visit my ailing father. That journey home taught me that some roads are worth traveling, no matter how far.

The words on this blog are written for my daughter as she grows up, for my future self, and for you, who have wandered here by chance.

If you like something you read, feel free to leave a comment.

— J. Gu, Torbay, Auckland, 2026
